Summary

Gabe Logan Newell (born November 3, 1962), also known by his nickname Gaben, is an American businessman who is the president and co-founder of the video game company Valve Corporation. He attended Harvard University in the early 1980s but dropped out to join Microsoft, where he helped create the first versions of the Windows operating system. He left Microsoft in 1996 to found Valve, and funded the development of his game, Half-Life. Newell led the development of Valve's digital distribution service, Steam, which was launched in 2003 and controlled most of the market for downloaded PC games by 2011.

Timeline

  • 1962: Newell was born on November 3, 1962, in Colorado, and attended Davis Senior High School in Davis, California.
  • 1980: He enrolled at Harvard University in 1980.
  • 1983: Meanwhile when Gabe was in College, He was convinced to drop of Harvard to work for Bill Gates at Microsoft by the head of sales in 1983. He spent 13 years at Microsoft as the producer of the first three releases of the Windows operation systems.
  • 1996: Gabe and another employee; Mike Harrington left Microsoft to found a video game company Valve on August, 24, 1996.
  • 1998: Gabe and Mike funded the development of thew first Valve game, the first-person shooter Half-Life, which became a critical and commercial success. Later on, Mike left Valve in 2000.
  • 2003: Gabe and Valve spent several months developing Steam, A video game digital distriubtion service and storefront. Later, released as a software client in September 2003.
  • 2004: Newell gave Valve no deadline and a "virtually unlimited" budget to develop Half-Life 2, He even promised fo fund it himself if necessary.
  • 2009: Newell was named by IGN as one of the top 100 game creators of all time, citing that it was "almost impossible to gauge" Valve's influence on game design, technology, and the video games industry.
  • 2010: Gabe was listed as "A Name You Need To Know" by Forbes, primarily for his work on Steam having partnerships with multiple major developers. He also won Pioneer award at the 2010 Game Developers Conference
  • 2013: Gabe was added to the Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences Hall of Fame and recieved the BAFTA Fellowship award for his contributions to the video game industry.
